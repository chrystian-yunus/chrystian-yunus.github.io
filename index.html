About

I am a M.Eng student in Informatics Engineering at Universitas Gadjah Mada, Indonesia. I am drawn to AI research because it combines abstract problem-solving with real-world impact. I enjoy exploring how computational methods can be designed not only to perform well, but also to be reliable, generalizable, and meaningful in practice. This perspective has guided my interest in both applied machine learning and the theoretical ideas that support intelligent systems.

Beyond my academic work, my experience in software testing and product design has influenced how I approach technology. It has made me more attentive to validation, user experience, and the gap between technical performance and actual system use. These experiences have also shaped my growing interest in human-computer interaction. In the long term, I hope to continue my academic journey through a PhD and contribute to research in AI and related fields.
